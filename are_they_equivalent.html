Are they equivalent?

SA: Fair Divisions

En Català!

 

My aunty and grandma can't agree on what portion of the dessert should be assigned to the cousins. They use the same recipe, with the same quantities, to make two pies that only differ in shape: the grandmother only had a round mold, and the aunt had a rectangular one. The grandmother wants to give the kids 2 parts of the 8 pieces she divided her pie into. The aunt cut hers into 12 pieces and wants to give us 3. They argue to see who is the most generous.
Don't they realize they are giving us equivalent portions?

Equivalent Fractions

Sometimes it happens that two fractions written differently represent the same number. For example, drinking "half a liter of water" is the same as "two quarters of a liter."

When two fractions represent the same number, we say that they are equivalent fractions.

How can we check if two fractions are equivalent? Normally, the quickest way is to check using this property:

Two fractions are equivalent if, when multiplied crosswise, the same number is obtained.

But there are also other methods. For example:

  • Simplifying the fractions and seeing if they have the same irreducible form (we'll see how to do this later).
  • Calculating the decimal expressions and checking if they are equal.

In the activity, you will be able to see different examples and how to solve them.
